What "AI Readiness" actually means

AI Readiness isn't about whether you use ChatGPT. It's about whether your organization is set up to actually benefit from AI – so it can save you time, find you funding, and give you leverage on the mission you're already running.

For that to happen, five things need to be in place:

  • Your data is usable. AI can't help if your donor list lives in someone's head.
  • You know where your time is going. Repetitive tasks you can name are tasks you can delegate.
  • You know your donors. Who they are, what they care about, and how to reach them. AI can't help you cultivate people you don't have a record of.
  • There's a clear way you do things. AI amplifies what's repeatable. If your methods live in one person's head, there's nothing to amplify yet.
  • You have momentum. You've tried a few things, you're curious, and you want to move. This is the input that makes the rest of the system actually get used.

The five conditions below are how we read where Kathy's Kids is today. The bigger the gap on any one condition, the more leverage a small change can unlock.

AI Grant & Donor Prospect Engine

Foundation · Direct answer to the magic wand

Your exact ask. Builds a workflow that uses AI to identify the foundations, corporations, and family offices most likely to fund the two areas Kathy's Kids supports – Northshore Education and developmental disability services. For each candidate, the workflow pulls giving history, board members, application windows, average grant size, and fit indicators, then prioritizes the top 20–50 prospects by likelihood of funding. The output is a refreshable monthly prospect list with everything you need to decide who to approach and when. This becomes your development pipeline. It would have taken a paid consultant weeks to assemble; once built, it runs in the background and updates on demand. This single module is worth the engagement on its own.

Donor CRM Lite

Foundation · Replaces "no usable list"

You told us "honestly, there may not be a usable list" – the lowest possible answer on that question, and the gap that pulls the data category down. This module gives Kathy's Kids a lightweight donor record sized for an organization at your scale: one row per donor, gift history, contact information, last touch, communication preferences, and board or volunteer involvement. The point is making "who do we have?" a five-second answer instead of a research project, and giving the prospects from Module 1 somewhere to land once they convert. Quick to build, immediate to use, and sized to fit a 501(c)(3) operating on tight budgets.

3

AI-Assisted Grant Writing System

Time leverage · Highest-effort task

Grant writing is the highest-effort task in any small foundation's calendar. This module builds a library of Kathy's Kids' past grant applications, the Northshore Education Consortium and Bass River program details, impact stories, and budget templates – then uses that material as context for AI to draft new applications. You review, personalize, and submit, instead of starting from a blank page each time. The target is roughly 80% AI draft, 20% your review – which is where the real time savings live. Cuts grant prep from days to hours, while keeping every application in the foundation's voice and tied to the mission's specific story. The pipeline from Module 1 feeds this directly: prospects get drafted toward, applications get tracked, the cycle compounds.
